# Design Law 4 — Service and Evidence

**Credibility is earned in the real world.**

Inherits from [`underspecified-surface.md`](./underspecified-surface.md).

---

## The law

A child gains standing in the village by doing something for another person in the real world, gathering evidence of the act, and bringing the evidence back into the village.

Standing is not gained by posting, streaking, being funny, being loud, having followers, or being seen. Standing is gained by **acts of service, evidenced.**

The service is real. The evidence is real. The credit is real.

---

## Why

Every existing children's platform rewards the child for the child's own performance — content produced for the platform, attention captured on the platform, time spent on the platform. The reward is entirely internal to the platform, entirely about the self, and entirely disconnected from the child's real community.

The village refuses this. The reward is external. It comes from what the child does **outside** the village, for **someone else**, and the village's only role is to witness it and record it.

This inverts the direction of formation. A child on the current substrate is being trained to look inward for value ("what will make me visible?"). A child in the village is being trained to look outward ("who around me could use help?"). Over years, that inversion is the difference between two very different adults.

---

## The evidence loop

Four steps. Same shape as the game's core loop, applied to the social surface.

1. **See a need.** The child notices someone around them who could use help. An elderly neighbor with a lawn to rake. A younger sibling who needs a walk to school. A newcomer at school who is eating lunch alone.
2. **Act.** The child does the thing. Rakes the lawn. Walks the sibling. Sits with the newcomer.
3. **Gather evidence.** Before and after. Photos, short video, voice notes. Whatever documents the act honestly.
4. **Return.** The child brings the evidence into the village. It is logged. It is witnessed. The child receives credit.

The loop teaches the child how to see, how to act, how to document, and how to complete the cycle. All four are skills. All four are being taught deliberately.

---

## Verification

Evidence without verification is gameable. The village handles this through the community that already surrounds the child.

- **Recipient confirmation.** The person helped confirms the act. A voice message from the elderly neighbor. A tap from a parent. A note from a teacher.
- **Trusted-adult witness.** If the recipient cannot confirm directly, a trusted adult in the child's life — parent, teacher, coach, clergy, family friend — marks the act witnessed.
- **No confirmation, no credit.** A child who cannot get their act confirmed does not receive credit. This is not a punishment. It is the honesty of the system.

The community verifies. The platform does not. The village trusts the adults who already surround the child more than it trusts any algorithm the Operator could write.

---

## Weighting

Not all acts of service carry the same weight. The village weights acts by the circumstances of the person helped.

- **Helping someone with fewer resources than you** — heavier weight.
- **Helping someone who cannot easily reciprocate** — heavier weight.
- **Helping someone the child does not know well** — heavier weight than helping close family (though family help still counts).
- **Consistency over time** — a small act repeated weekly is weighted more than a large one-time gesture.

At v1, weighting is done by **category** — the child records what kind of act it was (helping an elderly person, helping someone with a disability, helping a younger child, helping a newcomer) and the village applies the weight. The child never ranks specific people as "least fortunate." The category system does the weighting invisibly.

Later, when the community verification layer matures, the recipient's own circumstances can inform the weight silently — never shown to the child, never disclosed, but shaping the credit the child receives. The child learns which kinds of acts score highest by doing them and noticing the pattern. This is how virtue actually gets internalized: not by being told, but by being formed.

---

## What credit unlocks

Credit is not a leaderboard. Credit is a **currency of trust** inside the village. Higher-credit children can:

- Send messages that reach more people (Law 5 — earned broadcast).
- Post to the shared surface with priority — their evidence appears higher in the feed of children looking at what others have done.
- Access new tools in the village — the ability to build a larger fort, invite others in, create shared spaces, propose new places.
- Vouch for other children's evidence when the community verification layer is thin.

Credit is spent by being trusted. It is not spent by being ranked.

---

## What this refuses

- It refuses to reward performance for the platform's own sake.
- It refuses to reward the child for being seen, being funny, being loud, or being popular.
- It refuses to display a global leaderboard.
- It refuses to accept evidence without a real act behind it.
- It refuses to let a child rank specific people as "less fortunate."
- It refuses to publicize the weighting to the child — the child sees only that acts of service score, and learns the shape over time.

---

## What this commits to

- Standing in the village is earned outside the village, in the real world, for real people.
- Every act must have evidence, and every piece of evidence must be verifiable by a real adult in the child's life.
- The direction of formation is outward: from the child, toward the community around them.
- The child learns what virtue looks like by practicing it, not by being told about it.

---

## The generational stake

Children who spend seven years being trained to look for who around them needs help — and who are given real credit, in a system their peers respect, for helping — will grow into adults who look outward as a reflex. That reflex is the substrate of civil society. It is the thing the current cohort is being trained *out* of by platforms that reward inward-facing performance.

The village trains the opposite reflex. On the same device that would otherwise train the harmful one.

**Pour le bien-être du peuple.**
